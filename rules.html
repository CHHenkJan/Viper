The 5 VIPER Rules:
Why Discipline Beats Intuition in Scalping

01

Every trade has a stop loss.

This is not optional. It is the most important rule in scalping — and the most frequently violated. Without a hard stop loss, a single bad trade can wipe out 10 winning trades. VIPER sets the stop loss level before the entry price. Every time. No exceptions.

The math: At 0.5% risk per trade, you need to lose 20 consecutive trades to lose 10% of capital. That is survivable. Without a stop, one trade can do it.
02

0.5% maximum risk per scalp.

Position sizing is how you survive long enough to profit. VIPER uses fixed fractional sizing — risking exactly 0.5% of current portfolio value per trade. This ensures that losses are proportionate and the account can recover.

No "doubling up" on high-conviction setups. Conviction is not an edge. Consistency is.

03

Maximum 3 simultaneous positions.

Concentration risk is real, even in scalping. If three positions are open, no new entries are taken — regardless of signal quality. This caps total portfolio exposure at 1.5% at any given moment.

More positions means more monitoring, more slippage risk, and more room for correlation to create clustered losses. Three is the limit.

04

Volume must confirm every entry.

A signal without volume is a signal without conviction. VIPER requires current-period volume to exceed the 20-period volume average before entering a position. This filters out low-liquidity setups where slippage can destroy the edge.

Volume confirmation is the difference between trading a real move and trading noise. Thin markets mean wide spreads, slow fills, and unpredictable exits. VIPER does not trade in thin markets.

05

Time-based exit at 4 hours — no exceptions.

Scalping is about speed. If a trade hasn't hit its target within 4 hours, the thesis is invalidated. The position is closed at market — whether in profit, at break-even, or at a loss.

This prevents scalp trades from becoming swing trades, which changes the risk profile entirely. A 5-minute setup does not belong in a 24-hour hold. Time is a risk factor, and VIPER treats it as such.
